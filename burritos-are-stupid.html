Tacos vs. Burritos

5. You already know who wins, in a salsa landslide

Smothered in love

Team Taco won a long time ago, but for you pour souls who still believe in the return of Team Burrito, we wanted to rub it in even more. There's no such thing as someone who likes tacos and burritos equally. It's a victory lap of the top five reasons why tacos are better than burritos.

Taco Fun Fact: The largest flour taco in the world weighed 750 kg (1,654 lb) and was made by the city of Mexicali and Cocinex SA de CV, in Mexicali, Baja California, Mexico on March 8th, 2003. - Guinness World Records

5. Proper customization — You're already salivating thinking about squeezing those limes atop the holiest of foods. Add any topping you want at any point in the meal.

4. Flavor spotlight — Keep your worthless empty calorie rice and beans. Tacos highlight the single most ingredient without the need for any fluff. You also don't have to worry about "flavor fragmentation", where you get a mouthful of unevenly distributed brown rice more undesirable than a "Mike Tyson bite".

3. Ability to have an afternoon — Whatever part of the day you had left is gone nursing a burrito baby in your tummy. Burritos aren't considered a snack food for a reason, they're one size fits all cement brick in your stomach waiting to happen.

2. No distractions — Each individual taco is a perfect expression of itself.

1. Freedom of choice — Because we all have commitment issues, why tie yourself down to a single item outside your control. Sample one of each option, don't lock your tastebuds down. You can order one-hundred tacos or a single one.
